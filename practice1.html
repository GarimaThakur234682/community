History

An orphanage is a facility that provides shelter, food, education, healthcare, and emotional support to children who have lost one or both parents or have been abandoned. These children are often vulnerable and face numerous challenges, including lack of basic needs, loneliness, and social stigma. Orphanages strive to create a safe and stable environment for these children, with the ultimate goal of finding them permanent homes through adoption or reunification with their families. The staff of orphanages often includes social workers, psychologists, and caretakers who provide the children with comfort, guidance, and love.
